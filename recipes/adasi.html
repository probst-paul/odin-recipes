Adasi (Persian Lentil Soup)

Description

Adasi is a traditional Persian lentil dish known for its warmth, simplicity, and comforting flavor. Made with slow-cooked lentils, caramelized onions, and a blend of turmeric and spices, this version adds a drizzle of olive oil and a squeeze of lemon for extra depth. It’s a hearty, wholesome, and flavorful meal that's perfect for breakfast, lunch, or a light dinner—especially with warm flatbread on the side.

Ingredients

  • Green or brown lentils – 1 cup (rinsed)
  • Onion – 1 large, finely chopped
  • Garlic – 2 cloves, minced
  • Potato – 1 medium, peeled and diced (optional but traditional)
  • Turmeric – 1 tsp
  • Cumin – ½ tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Black pepper – to taste
  • Olive oil – 2 tbsp
  • Water or vegetable broth – 4 cups
  • Lemon juice – 1 tbsp (optional, for serving)
  • Flatbread – for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tbsp of olive oil in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden and caramelized, about 10–12 minutes.
  3. Add minced garlic, turmeric, and cumin. Stir for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add lentils, diced potatoes, and water or broth. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30–40 minutes, or until lentils and potatoes are tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Drizzle with remaining olive oil and a squeeze of lemon juice before serving, if desired.
  8. Serve hot with flatbread on the side.
